# InterviewOS — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Betty UX research teams — researchers who run studies, recruit and manage participants, schedule sessions, and coordinate comms. Internal tool; access restricted to betty.com / uk.betty.com / playbetty.co.uk. Users are researchers, not engineers — they care about getting things done quickly and clearly.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, playful, human.** Betty is not a cold enterprise product. It should feel like a thoughtful colleague, not a clinical database.

Three words: **warm · human · considered**

Emotional goals: confidence (I know where things are), calm (nothing feels overwhelming), delight (small moments of joy in a workaday tool).

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light only
- **Primary palette**: Warm amber (oklch hue 86) — not indigo, not blue
- **Typography**: DM Serif Text for headings (loaded via index.html), Inter for body
- **Anti-references**: Salesforce, Airtable-enterprise, cold-grey SaaS dashboards, anything clinical or overly structured
- **References**: Linear (clarity + craft), Notion (human text), a warm editorial feel
- **Radius**: Rounded but not bubbly — 8px base feels right for warmth without being toy-like

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over sterility** — prefer amber tints, rounded corners, and DM Serif headings over clinical grays and sharp edges
2. **Clarity at speed** — researchers glance at this tool between sessions; every label, status, and number should be instantly readable
3. **Consistent tokens** — use `var(--primary)` OKLCH tokens directly (never wrap in `hsl()`); use Tailwind semantic classes over hardcoded colors
4. **Interactions earn trust** — every hover, focus, and transition should feel intentional; 150–200ms ease-out, never bouncy
5. **Empty states invite, not apologise** — empty states should feel like an invitation to start, not a blank void
